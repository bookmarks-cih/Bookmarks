Why it matters

It turns Bookmarks into a map of trusted internet ecosystems.

Instead of only listing resources, Bookmarks becomes a place where expertise, taste, context, and identity shape discovery. That makes experience more human, more niche, and far more memorable than a plain directory.

Human context

Links feel stronger when users understand why they were selected and who stands behind them. Stacks provide narrative, trust, and editorial framing.

Better discovery

A person-driven browsing model helps users reach precise subcultures and useful niches more quickly than broad taxonomy alone ever could.

Creator value

It gives creators a clean public page for their best resources, with attribution, direct traffic, and a better way to organize useful internet.

Designed for human collaboration, not lazy scraping.

Bookmarks aims to feature resources through voluntary contribution, curator-approved selections, or assisted curation workflows that stay respectful, editable, and clearly attributed.

Official attribution: every collection can carry clear curator credit and profile links.
Flexible input: exports, simple lists, docs, spreadsheets, or rough drafts are all workable.
Editorial control: curators can request edits, updates, or removal at any time.

Purpose

Bookmarks exists to turn scattered internet knowledge into curated, readable public collections. The goal is not just to list links, but to organize useful resources with context, attribution, and a cleaner discovery experience.

How submissions work

When someone submits a profile, list, or recommendation, we review the material, check relevance, and shape it into a more structured public page when it fits the project.

Safety & trust

We care about accuracy, source quality, and respectful representation. Reports about broken links, impersonation, or attribution issues can be sent directly through this contact channel.
