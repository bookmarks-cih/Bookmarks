Bookmarks News

Stay up to date with the latest features, improvements, and announcements from the Bookmarks team.

March 9, 2026 Feature Update & Community

New Sections Added: Civil Rights & Psychology in Bookmarks Docs

We are excited to announce the addition of two new sections to the Docs area of Bookmarks: "Civil Rights" and "Psychology". These categories have been carefully curated to provide valuable resources and documentation on important social justice and mental health topics.

This update reflects our ongoing commitment to expanding access to knowledge and supporting diverse educational needs within our community.

March 8, 2026 Technical Update & Community

Performance Overhaul & Special Wishes

We have significantly improved the homepage and hub by reducing animation lag and simplifying navigation and responsiveness on mobile devices. To support these enhancements, we have integrated new libraries such as AlpineJS, ThreeJS, GSAP, and AnimeJS.

Additionally, we have completed the graphic redesign of our HTTPing, however this service will be paused for the foreseeable future due to a lack of support, making us unable to cover the necessary runtime API costs.

On a lighter note, we wish a joyful International Women's Day 2026 to all the women in our community and around the world. Your strength and creativity continue to inspire us all.

March 6, 2026 Feature Release

Introducing Dump Status Dashboard & CSV Access

We are excited to announce the launch of our new Dump Status page, a dedicated dashboard designed to provide full transparency on the health of the Bookmarks repository. You can now monitor the status of thousands of links in real-time, checking for active connections, redirect chains, and DNS errors. These status reports are generated daily between 12:00 and 18:00 (French time) to ensure up-to-date accuracy.

For developers and data enthusiasts who prefer raw data, we have also made the entire archive accessible via CSV files on GitHub. You can explore the datasets or integrate them into your own tools directly from our GitHub Repository. We hope these additions provide greater utility and reliability for the community.

May 24, 2025 Feature Update

Multi-Category Selection & Display Optimization

We have introduced a highly requested feature: the ability to select multiple categories simultaneously. This allows for more precise filtering and a more flexible browsing experience tailored to your specific needs.

To ensure a cleaner and more minimalist interface, a "Show More" button has been added to the category list. This optimization hides the bulk of the categories by default, reducing visual clutter and keeping the focus on the main content.

May 22, 2025 Feature Update

Interface Refinements & Mobile Optimization

We've rolled out major visual updates to the main dashboard, focusing on atmosphere refinement with layered gradients and enhanced "glassmorphism" effects on cards for improved readability.

Most importantly, we've completely overhauled the mobile experience: all pages now feature a modern, fully responsive design built to be truly phone-friendly. Navigation, layout, and interactions are optimized for seamless use on smartphones and tablets — expect smoother transitions and a more consistent dark/light theme balance across all devices.

01. Recent Updates

Project Changelog

Track the evolution of Bookmarks with our latest feature releases, performance improvements, and community announcements.

March 9, 2026

Civil Rights and Psychology Went Live

Two new documentation lanes were added to Bookmarks: Civil Rights and Psychology. This release expands the platform beyond pure tooling and research by making room for social, legal, and mental-health oriented reading paths.

March 8, 2026

Homepage Performance Pass Completed

We reworked the homepage and hub interactions to feel lighter, cleaner, and less noisy on slower devices. Animation timing, navigation flow, and general visual balance were refined to make browsing smoother across the site.

March 6, 2026

Dump Status Dashboard Published

The new Dump Status page is now part of the ecosystem, giving visitors a clearer view of archive health and link maintenance. We also opened a CSV-oriented workflow for users who want a more raw, data-first view of the collection.

February 25, 2026

News Page Reframed as a Product Log

The News area was repositioned to work as a readable timeline for Bookmarks itself. Instead of acting like a generic feed, it now better reflects product changes, section launches, and archive milestones.

February 18, 2026

Creator Profiles Expanded Into a Real Directory

We moved beyond a placeholder creator concept and pushed a more complete profile system with dedicated pages, curated resource cards, and clearer attribution. The goal was to make each featured profile feel like a navigable knowledge space instead of a simple shoutout.

February 10, 2026

Bookmark Detail Pages Added for Key Resources

Several direct resource pages were introduced so visitors can land on a single bookmark and immediately understand why it matters. This helped the archive feel less like a raw list and more like a guided reference system.

January 28, 2026

Hub Layout Became the Main Navigation Layer

The hub was upgraded into a central routing surface for the project. Larger cards, stronger hierarchy, and a more visual tile system made it easier to jump between docs, files, creators, jobs, and support pages.

January 14, 2026

Docs Page Structure Was Reorganized

We cleaned up the Docs experience so categories feel more intentional and easier to scan. This pass focused on improving the way long-term knowledge collections are presented, especially for users exploring multiple subjects in one session.

December 19, 2025

Files Space Introduced for Shared Assets

A dedicated Files section was added to support downloadable resources, packaged material, and structured asset access. This gave Bookmarks a more practical layer for users who need more than just external links.

December 3, 2025

Edit Workflow Entered the Interface

We introduced a visible edit pathway to support future curation updates and content management actions. Even in its early form, the edit area marked a shift toward Bookmarks as a living system rather than a static gallery.

November 21, 2025

Jobs Section Joined the Platform Map

The Jobs page was added as part of a broader effort to connect discovery with opportunity. It gives the project a more career-oriented angle and helps extend the platform from research and reading into practical next steps.

November 8, 2025

Theme Switching Was Polished Across Core Pages

We improved the dark and light theme behavior so the site feels more consistent from page to page. Small visual mismatches were reduced, and the overall interface now carries a stronger product identity regardless of theme.

October 26, 2025

Footer and Global Navigation Were Unified

Navigation patterns were normalized across the site to reduce friction and repetition. This included updates to footer links, menu structure, and the way supporting pages connect back to the main archive.

October 12, 2025

Support and Trust Pages Were Filled Out

Pages covering privacy, terms, support, and public-facing project framing were expanded so the platform feels more complete and credible. This was an important step in turning Bookmarks into a publishable product instead of a private prototype.

September 28, 2025

Safety and Prevention Category Added

We added a Safety and Prevention lane to make room for resources focused on well-being, awareness, and personal resilience. That update broadened the editorial scope of Bookmarks while staying aligned with the idea of useful, structured internet.

September 10, 2025

Public Messaging for Creators Was Clarified

We refined how the project speaks to contributors, creators, and experts interested in being featured. The messaging now better explains attribution, editorial control, and how public resource pages can benefit both visitors and contributors.

August 22, 2025

Prototype Concepts Became Production Directions

Several ideas that originally lived inside prototype pages were promoted into the main product direction. This included clearer language around curation, audience value, and the role of themed knowledge pathways inside the site.

July 30, 2025

Data Library Continued to Grow

The main bookmarks dataset was expanded with more categories, more references, and better thematic spread. This work is less visible than a homepage redesign, but it remains the foundation of what makes the platform useful.

June 18, 2025

Card Styling and Glass UI Were Refined

We tightened spacing, softened contrast, and improved the layered glass treatment used across key panels. These changes were made to keep the interface expressive without sacrificing readability.

April 27, 2025

Archive Positioning Became More Curatorial

Bookmarks started leaning more clearly into curation rather than simple aggregation. The writing, structure, and page relationships were updated to make it obvious that selection quality matters as much as quantity.

February 2, 2025

Foundations for the Public Bookmarks Hub Were Set

Early layout work established the core direction of the project: a curated internet hub with sections, profiles, expandable knowledge areas, and a visual identity strong enough to support long-term growth.

Bookmarks — Curated internet, structured with intent.
